What to do if I don’t know where to start?

Imagine you are someone else that is trying to answer this question. By putting yourself in various roles (e.g. teenage social media user, lonely elderly person, target demographic of social prejudice), you may be able to move on from “what are my ideas” to what is the possible impact of such an augmented reality system crossed with social media on greater society.

Do I have to come up with the ideas by myself?

We ask you to not discuss your ideas with anyone else you may know who is a part of this user study. However, feel free to discuss with friends and family members which may help give you new ideas.

What do I do if I feel stuck?

If imagining you are someone else and talking to others haven’t helped, move on to another image in your brainstorming diary. Otherwise, take a break from your booklet or if it helps. You can also reach out to us via email and we can set up a meeting to get your ideas unstuck.

How can I brainstorm on location?

Take a look around on location and gather your impressions of the space such as the sounds, smells, the people, shapes, the calmness or business of the environment, as well as your general feelings and thoughts you might have as you are in that space. After “absorbing” these surroundings, scan the prompts in order and see which ones spark some sort of idea you may have and try to capture that within your booklet.

What if I am not able to brainstorm on location?

Familiarize yourself with the prompts. The next time you find yourself at the location without your booklet, spend a few fleeting moments capturing observations about the environment (see FAQ: How can I best brainstorm on location?). Take notes, video, or audio recording of your impressions. With these references and impressions, you can later come back to your booklet with a richer understanding of the place and approach brainstorming a few of the prompts for that particular image.

Do I really have to visit the location of the image again?

No, not necessarily, but there will be a difference in your brainstorm if you do with some ideas present that you might not have considered before. If you already have ideas about a particular location, go ahead and write/draw them down. This booklet isn’t meant to restrict your ideas, rather help guide the formation of new ideas you may not have easily had before.

How do I use the materials?

You don’t have to use the materials like sticky notes or stickers if they don’t aid you creatively. When you feel that your ideas are limited just by representing them by text or drawing, keep the materials in mind. You can even feel free to use your own stationary or materials!
